What the terms mean

Score
0–90 scale combining chart patterns, trend strength, and volume signals. Higher means stronger short-term technical setup.
EMA50 / EMA200
Average price over the last 50 or 200 days. Price above these lines usually means the stock is in an uptrend.
Golden cross
When the EMA50 crosses above the EMA200 — a classic long-term bullish signal.
RSI
Measures whether a stock is overbought or oversold, on a scale of 0–100. Mid-range (40–65) is healthy; above 75 means stretched.
MACD crossover
A momentum signal. A bullish crossover often appears just as upward momentum is building.
ADX
Measures how strong the current trend is, regardless of direction. Above 25 means a real trend (not just choppy sideways action).
OBV (On-Balance Volume)
Tracks cumulative buying vs selling pressure. Rising OBV while price stays flat can mean quiet accumulation by big buyers.
Hammer / Engulfing / Morning star
Candlestick patterns that have historically appeared near short-term price bottoms — they signal a possible reversal upward.
Breakout
When price moves above a recent ceiling (like a 20-day high), especially on heavy volume, suggesting fresh upward momentum.
Penny stocks
Here, this just means price below ₹50 — a simple price cutoff, not an official classification, since real index/fundamental data isn't available from this data source.
